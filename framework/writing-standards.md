# Writing Standards

Every devotional in every series must meet these standards.

## The non-negotiables

Every devotional should:

- **Glorify Jesus Christ.** He is the center, never a footnote.
- **Teach Scripture accurately in context.** Never twist a verse to fit a point.
- **Be practical before being inspirational.** Inspiration without action fades.
- **Move from revelation to application.** Always land on "what must I do?"
- **Be memorable enough to retell.** If it can't be repeated, it won't be remembered.
- **Include stories and illustrations.** Children understand stories; adults remember illustrations.
- **Speak to children, youth, adults, and older believers.** One message, all ages.
- **End with hope and action.** Never leave the listener in despair or passivity.

## Scripture handling

- Default translation: **KJV** (matches the established series voice). Quote accurately,
  with book, chapter, and verse.
- One primary passage per episode; a second supporting passage is allowed. Avoid
  stacking many references in the main flow — put extra cross-references under
  "Supporting Scriptures."
- When citing a character, anchor to the text (e.g., Caleb — Joshua 14:10–12).

## Theological guardrails

- Keep the gospel central: creation, fall, redemption in Christ, restoration.
- Long life / blessing teaching must avoid prosperity distortion — God's promises serve
  His purpose and glory, not human comfort or accumulation.
- Present faith as relationship with God before religious activity.
- Hold both truths: a long life lived for God *and* the eternal value of any life in
  Christ. Never imply that an early death signals lesser faith or favor.

## Tone & accessibility

- Warm, direct, pastoral. Speak *to* the listener, not *about* the topic.
- Short sentences. Generous white space. One idea per line where it aids retention.
- Define or avoid jargon. A new believer and a child should both follow it.
- Practical applications must be concrete and doable *today*.
