# Style Guide (The Canon)

This is the single source of truth for **voice, formatting, and the episode template**.
Every episode file must follow it so all 23 read as one series. When in doubt, match
Episode 1.

---

## Voice

- **Pastoral, warm, direct.** Speak *to* the listener ("you"), not *about* the topic.
- **Short lines, generous white space.** One idea per line where it aids retention. This
  is built to be *spoken* and *retold*, not just read.
- **Scripture is KJV** unless a series specifies otherwise. Quote accurately with book,
  chapter, verse.
- **All ages at once.** A child should grasp the story; an elder should find the depth.
- **Hope and action always.** Never end flat. End with a challenge or a charge.
- **Christ-centered.** Jesus is the destination of every teaching, not a closing line.

### Formatting conventions

- Use `>` blockquotes for Scripture and for the Big Idea.
- **Bold** key phrases sparingly — the line that should survive in memory.
- Use the short-line cadence shown in the template below (line breaks as breath marks).
- Emoji are allowed only in Short headers and series-branding lines (matching the
  established style, e.g. ⏱️ 🌳 📖). Keep the teaching body clean.

## The Episode Template

Every episode file uses this exact section order. Sections marked *(optional)* are
included when the material supports them.

```markdown
# EPISODE N
## {Episode Title}

> **Series:** {Series Name} — Week {n}: {Week Theme}

### Key Scripture
> "{verse text}"
> — {Reference (KJV)}

### Hook
{30–60s of spoken copy. "What if…" / "Did you know…" / "Imagine…" / a statistic or
question. Short lines. Earns attention in the first sentence.}

### Big Idea
> {One sentence. Everything in the episode revolves around this.}

### Why This Matters
{2–4 short lines connecting the hook to the listener's life.}

### Teaching
#### 1. {Point title}
{Biblical explanation → supporting Scripture → practical application.}
**Supporting Scriptures:** {refs}

#### 2. {Point title}
...

#### 3. {Point title}
...
{3–5 points total.}

### Biblical Example
{At least one Bible character, anchored to the text.}

### Illustration
{One memorable illustration from nature, family, business, sports, history, science,
agriculture, construction, or technology.}

### Application
**What should I do today?**
{Concrete, doable today. Bullet or short lines.}

### Reflection Questions   *(optional)*
- {question}
- {question}

### Prayer
{Simple, Christ-centred, faith-filled. First person.}

### Weekly Challenge
{One action to practise this week. (May repeat across a week — that's intentional.)}

### Longevity Insight
> {A ~60s takeaway connecting Scripture to a practical observation from everyday life or
> science. This recurring feature is part of the series' identity. Rename per series if
> the theme differs — e.g. "Wisdom Insight".}

- **Quote graphic:** {the one line to put on an image}

- **Discussion prompt:** {one question for groups}
```

## The Short series branding

For *Secrets of Longevity*, every Short opens with:

> "Welcome to **Secrets of Longevity in 60 Seconds**."

(Alternate titles on file: *60 Seconds to Live Well* · *One Minute Wisdom for Long Life*.)
Pick one per series and use it consistently.

---

## File naming

- Episodes: `week-{w}-{theme}/episode-{NN}-{kebab-title}.md` (NN zero-padded: 01, 02 …).
- Shorts: `shorts/short-{NN}-{kebab-title}.md`.
- Keep the episode number aligned across the episode file, its Short, and its discussion
  guide.

---

## Quality bar (self-check before marking an episode done)

- [ ] Opens with a genuine hook, not a definition.
- [ ] Exactly one primary Scripture (two max).
- [ ] Big Idea is one sentence and is the spine of the episode.
- [ ] 3–5 teaching points, each with Scripture + application.
- [ ] At least one Bible character, anchored to the text.
- [ ] One vivid illustration.
- [ ] Application is doable *today*.
- [ ] Christ is central; no prosperity distortion.
- [ ] Companion Short is a *fresh hook*, not a summary.
- [ ] Reads aloud well in the series cadence.
