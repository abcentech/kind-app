# EPISODE 6
## Love God Wholeheartedly

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 2: Faith (*Strengthening the Spirit*)

### Key Scripture
> "Because he hath set his love upon me, therefore will I deliver him: I will set him on
> high, because he hath known my name."
> — Psalm 91:14 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Read Psalm 91 carefully and you'll find a hinge.

For thirteen verses, God is the One acting — covering, sheltering, delivering, protecting.

Then verse 14 turns, and for the first time the man does something.

What does he do?

He doesn't perform a ritual.

He doesn't recite a formula.

"*Because he hath set his love upon me...*"

He loves God.

And out of that love flows every promise in the chapter — including the great one: *"With long life will I satisfy him."*

---

### Big Idea
> Long, satisfied life is rooted not in religious activity but in wholehearted love for God.

---

### Why This Matters
You can do everything right and still miss the one thing.

You can attend, serve, give, and recite — and never love.

God is not first looking for your performance. He is looking for your heart.

Religion can be sustained for a while on willpower. But only love lasts a lifetime — and only love makes that lifetime satisfying.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. God Wants Your Whole Heart, Not Part of It
"Thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy might" (Deuteronomy 6:5).

All. All. All.

God does not ask for a corner of your heart. He asks for the whole of it.

Jesus called this "the first and great commandment" (Matthew 22:37–38). Not the first of many equals — the first in rank, the foundation everything else stands on.

A divided heart is a draining heart. Wholehearted love is the only love that does not exhaust you.

**Application:** Name the one rival competing for your heart's first place today, and consciously hand it back to God.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Deuteronomy 6:5; Matthew 22:37–38; Proverbs 23:26

#### 2. Relationship Comes Before Religion
God's first words to Israel before any law were "I am the LORD thy God" — relationship before requirement.

Religion asks, "What must I do?" Love asks, "How can I please the One I love?"

The same actions — prayer, giving, gathering — can flow from cold duty or from warm love. God receives the second and is wearied by the first. "This people draweth nigh unto me with their mouth... but their heart is far from me" (Matthew 15:8).

You were not saved into a system. You were saved into a relationship with a Person.

**Application:** Before any spiritual habit today, pause and remember *Whom* you are doing it for. Turn the task back into a relationship.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Exodus 20:2; Matthew 15:8; Hosea 6:6

#### 3. Love Is the Root of a Long, Satisfied Life
Look again at Psalm 91:14. The love comes first; the promises follow.

"*Because* he hath set his love upon me, *therefore* will I deliver him."

The deliverance, the honor, the long life and salvation of verse 16 — all of it grows from one root: a man who set his love upon God.

A long life without love for God is merely many years. Love is what makes the years *satisfying* rather than merely long.

**Application:** Stop chasing the promises and start cultivating the love. Tell God you love Him today — out loud, on purpose.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 91:14–16; Psalm 63:3; 1 Corinthians 2:9

#### 4. We Love Because He First Loved Us
Wholehearted love is not something you manufacture. It is something you receive and return.

"We love him, because he first loved us" (1 John 4:19).

The cross is the measure of His love — "God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us" (Romans 5:8). When you see how much you are loved, love rises in you as a response.

Don't strain to love God more. Sit longer in how much He loves you, and your love will grow on its own.

**Application:** Spend a few minutes today simply remembering the cross. Let His love for you fuel your love for Him.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 John 4:19; Romans 5:8; John 3:16

---

### Biblical Example
- **David** is called "a man after God's own heart" (1 Samuel 13:14) — flawed, but in love with God: "my soul thirsteth for thee" (Psalm 63:1).
- **Mary of Bethany** sat at Jesus' feet while Martha was "cumbered about much serving," and Jesus said she had chosen the good part (Luke 10:38–42).
- **Peter** was restored not by a list of duties but by one question, asked three times: "Lovest thou me?" (John 21:15–17).

Jesus could have asked Peter, "Will you work harder?" He didn't. He went straight to the root: *Do you love me?* Everything else flows from the answer.

---

### Illustration
Picture a marriage held together only by chores.

The bills are paid. The dishes are done. The schedule runs like clockwork. On paper, everything functions.

But there is no affection. No delight. No love. Just two people performing tasks side by side.

It is technically a marriage — and it is quietly dying.

That is religion without love. All the duties done, the heart gone cold.

**A modern picture:** A plant can be watered, fertilized, and given perfect light — but cut it off from its root and it will wilt within days, no matter how good the care. **Love for God is the root. Cut from it, even the most disciplined religious life slowly withers; joined to it, the simplest life flourishes for decades.**

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Tell God you love Him — out loud, before you do anything for Him.
- Name and surrender the rival competing for your heart's first place.
- Turn one religious "task" today back into a relationship.
- Sit a few minutes in His love for you at the cross, and let yours rise in answer.

---

### Reflection Questions
- Do I love God, or do I only serve Him out of habit?
- What is competing for the first place in my heart?
- When did I last simply enjoy God, rather than work for Him?

---

### Prayer
Father,

I don't want religion without relationship.

I don't want to serve You with cold hands and a far-off heart.

Forgive me for the ways I have performed without love.

Help me to love You with all my heart, all my soul, all my might.

You first loved me — let me love You in return,

all the days of my long life.

In Jesus' Name,

Amen.

### Longevity Insight
> The longest, most thorough study of adult life ever conducted spent decades searching for what makes a life satisfying, and landed on one word: love — warm, committed relationship. Scripture said it first. God does not promise merely *long* life in Psalm 91; He promises to *satisfy* you with it. And the satisfaction begins exactly where the psalm says it does — with a heart that has set its love upon Him.
