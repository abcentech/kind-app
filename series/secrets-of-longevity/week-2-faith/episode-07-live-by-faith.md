# EPISODE 7
## Live by Faith

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 2: Faith (*Strengthening the Spirit*)

### Key Scripture
> "Now the just shall live by faith: but if any man draw back, my soul shall have no
> pleasure in him."
> — Hebrews 10:38 (KJV)

---

### Hook
At eighty-five, most people are slowing down.

Caleb was asking for a mountain.

Forty-five years earlier, ten spies looked at giants and saw defeat. Two looked at the same giants and saw God.

Four decades later, while a whole generation had died in the wilderness, Caleb stood up — strong, sharp, undaunted — and said:

"Now therefore give me this mountain."

What kept him standing when everyone else lay down?

One thing.

Faith for the long haul.

---

### Big Idea
> Faith is not a single leap — it is the endurance that carries you across decades.

---

### Why This Matters
Anyone can believe for a moment.

The crowd believed at the Red Sea and complained three days later.

The question is not whether you can start by faith. The question is whether your faith can *last* — through delay, through wilderness, through forty silent years.

A long life for God is impossible without a faith that endures. "The just shall live by faith" — not flare and fade, but *live*.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Faith Believes God Before It Sees the Result
"Faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen" (Hebrews 11:1).

Faith acts on God's word before the proof arrives.

When God called Abraham, "he went out, not knowing whither he went" (Hebrews 11:8). No map. No guarantee he could see. Just God's promise and a willingness to move on it.

You will rarely see the whole staircase. Faith takes the first step in the dark because God said to.

**Application:** Identify one thing God has clearly told you to do that you've delayed for lack of proof. Take the first step by faith today.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Hebrews 11:1; Hebrews 11:8; 2 Corinthians 5:7

#### 2. Faith Holds On Through the Long Delay
Abraham waited twenty-five years for the son God promised — and "against hope believed in hope" (Romans 4:18).

Faith's hardest test is usually not the size of the obstacle but the length of the wait.

"He staggered not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was strong in faith, giving glory to God" (Romans 4:20). The years did not weaken him; they aged his faith like fine wine.

What God promises, He keeps — but often on a timeline that requires endurance. Faith is how you stay believing in the gap.

**Application:** Name the promise you're waiting on. Choose today to keep believing through the delay rather than drawing back.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Romans 4:18–21; Hebrews 6:15; Habakkuk 2:3

#### 3. Faith Refuses to Draw Back
"The just shall live by faith: but if any man draw back, my soul shall have no pleasure in him" (Hebrews 10:38).

There are only two directions in the life of faith: forward or backward.

The wilderness generation drew back at the border of the Promised Land — and spent forty years circling a grave. Faith is what keeps you from turning around when the destination is in sight but the giants are still there.

Endurance is not glamorous. It is simply refusing to quit on the God who has not quit on you.

**Application:** Wherever you are tempted to give up, draw a line today: *"I will not draw back."* Take one step forward instead.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Hebrews 10:38–39; Galatians 6:9; Numbers 14:24

#### 4. Faith Stays Strong to the Very End
Caleb at eighty-five: "As yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day that Moses sent me... give me this mountain" (Joshua 14:11–12).

Faith does not retire.

While a whole generation perished in unbelief, Caleb's faith kept him strong — body, spirit, and resolve — for decades. He "wholly followed the LORD" and outlasted them all (Joshua 14:14).

The goal is not a strong start. It is a faith still climbing mountains when the world expects you to sit down.

**Application:** Refuse the lie that your best days of faith are behind you. Ask God for one "mountain" to take in this season — and step toward it.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Joshua 14:10–14; Numbers 14:24; 2 Timothy 4:7

---

### Biblical Example
- **Abraham** left home not knowing where, waited 25 years for Isaac, and "staggered not at the promise of God" (Hebrews 11:8–12; Romans 4:18–21).
- **Caleb** followed God wholly for 45 years and at 85 asked for the mountain still held by giants (Joshua 14:6–14).
- **The wilderness generation** stands as the warning: they "could not enter in because of unbelief" (Hebrews 3:19).

Two men entered the Promised Land out of an entire generation. Both were old. Both were ordinary in every way but one: their faith did not draw back, and so it carried them all the way across.

---

### Illustration
Faith is less like a firework and more like a marathon.

A firework is spectacular — a single, brilliant burst — and then it's gone, ash on the wind.

A marathon is unglamorous. Mile after mile, the same plodding stride. No crowd for most of it. The runner's whole strategy is simply: *do not stop.* And it's the runner who refuses to stop, not the one with the flashiest first mile, who crosses the line.

Most people want faith like a firework. God is building faith like a marathon.

**A modern picture:** A bridge is not judged by how it looks on opening day, but by whether it still carries traffic in fifty years. Engineers build for the *long load*, not the first crossing. **God is building a faith in you designed to bear weight across decades — not a faith that looks impressive once and cracks under the second storm.**

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Take the first step on one thing God told you to do, even without proof.
- Name the promise you're waiting on, and choose to keep believing through the delay.
- Draw a line where you're tempted to quit: *"I will not draw back."*
- Ask God for a "mountain" in this season — and step toward it.

---

### Reflection Questions
- Where am I living by sight when God is calling me to live by faith?
- What delay is tempting me to draw back?
- Is my faith built like a firework or like a marathon?

Faith is what carries you across the decades.
