# EPISODE 4
## Feed Your Spirit

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 2: Faith (*Strengthening the Spirit*)

### Key Scripture
> "But he answered and said, It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by
> every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God."
> — Matthew 4:4 (KJV)

---

### Hook
You will eat three meals today without being told.

Your stomach will remind you.

It will growl. It will ache. It will demand to be fed.

But your spirit?

Your spirit can starve for weeks...

for months...

and never make a sound.

That is the danger.

The part of you that lives forever is the part most easily neglected.

---

### Big Idea
> What you feed grows, and what you starve dies — so feed your spirit daily on the Word, prayer, and worship.

---

### Why This Matters
A weak body can still serve God.

A weak mind can still be renewed.

But a starved spirit cannot sustain a long, fruitful life.

You cannot pour out for seventy years from a well you never fill.

The secret to lasting isn't found in lasting harder — it's found in feeding deeper.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. The Spirit Is Fed by the Word of God
Jesus, hungry after forty days, did not first reach for bread.

He reached for Scripture.

"Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God."

The Word is not decoration for your spirit — it is its daily bread. Peter calls it milk; Paul calls it meat; Job said he esteemed it more than his necessary food.

A Bible you only carry feeds no one. A Bible you open and obey feeds the deepest part of you.

**Application:** Open the Word before you open your phone today. Read until one verse feeds you, then carry it through the day.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Matthew 4:4; Jeremiah 15:16; 1 Peter 2:2; Job 23:12

#### 2. The Spirit Is Fed by Prayer
Prayer is how the spirit breathes.

It is not a performance for God to admire — it is a child speaking to a Father who listens.

When Jesus, the Son of God, needed strength, He withdrew to pray. If the One who never sinned needed prayer to be sustained, how much more do you?

A prayerless believer is a breathless believer — alive in name, faint in strength.

**Application:** Set one fixed time to pray each day this week. Not a long speech — an honest conversation. Begin with thanks, end with surrender.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Luke 5:16; 1 Thessalonians 5:17; Philippians 4:6–7

#### 3. The Spirit Is Fed by Worship
Worship turns your eyes off your problems and onto your God.

It is the spirit lifting its hands when the body is tired and the mind is heavy.

When Paul and Silas worshipped at midnight in a prison, chains did not stop them — and the prison could not hold them. Worship fed their spirits until the walls themselves gave way.

You were made to magnify God. A spirit that never worships shrinks; a spirit that worships grows large enough to carry decades of trouble.

**Application:** Before you touch a task today, give God five minutes of worship — sing, give thanks, declare His goodness out loud.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Acts 16:25; Psalm 100:1–2; John 4:24

#### 4. A Starved Spirit Cannot Sustain a Long Life
You can add years to a man and still lose the man.

Many live long in the body while their spirit grew faint and gave up decades ago.

The promise is not merely "long life" — it is long life *with God*, fruitful to the end. That requires a spirit fed every single day.

What you neglect today, you will need tomorrow. Feed now, so you can stand later.

**Application:** Examine your week honestly: when did you last feed your spirit on purpose? Start a daily rhythm today — small, but unbroken.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 92:13–14; Isaiah 40:31; Proverbs 4:23

---

### Biblical Example
- **Jesus** answered every temptation with "It is written," fed on Scripture in the wilderness (Matthew 4:1–11).
- **David** said, "O God, thou art my God; early will I seek thee... my soul thirsteth for thee" (Psalm 63:1).
- **Daniel** kept windows open toward Jerusalem and prayed three times a day, even on pain of death (Daniel 6:10) — and outlasted kings and kingdoms.

Daniel served under four reigns and survived a den of lions. His secret was not strategy. It was a spirit fed daily, kneeling at the same window, year after year.

---

### Illustration
Picture two campfires on a cold night.

One is fed — logs added before the embers fade, tended through the darkness. By morning it is still burning, still giving warmth.

The other is left alone. It blazes brightly for an hour, then nobody adds wood. The flame drops to embers, the embers to ash, and by midnight the cold wins.

Both fires started the same.

The difference was not the spark. The difference was what got fed.

**A modern picture:** Your phone can have the brightest screen and the fastest chip, but leave it off the charger and by evening it is a dead slab of glass. Your spirit is the same. **Plug in daily, or run out exactly when you need power most.**

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Feed your spirit *first* — before your phone, before the news, before email.
- Read until one verse feeds you, then pray it back to God.
- Give five minutes to worship before your tasks begin.
- Tonight, ask: *"Did I feed my spirit today, or only my body?"*

---

### Reflection Questions
- What have I been feeding most — my body, my mind, or my spirit?
- When did I last sit with God's Word and let it actually feed me?
- What one daily habit could keep my spirit fed for the long haul?

Keep my spirit strong all the days of my life,

that I may serve You to the very end.

### Weekly Challenge
This week, set aside 15 minutes each morning to feed your spirit — open the Word, pray, and worship before you touch your phone.

---

### Longevity Insight
> Doctors will tell you that what you eat daily shapes how long you live. The same law governs the spirit. The believers who finish strong at eighty are not the ones who had a powerful moment at twenty — they are the ones who fed their spirits every ordinary day in between. Small meals, taken faithfully, outlast every feast you forget to repeat.
