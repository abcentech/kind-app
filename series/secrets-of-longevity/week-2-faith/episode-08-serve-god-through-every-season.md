# EPISODE 8
## Serve God Through Every Season

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 2: Faith (*Strengthening the Spirit*)

### Key Scripture
> "They shall still bring forth fruit in old age; they shall be fat and flourishing."
> — Psalm 92:14 (KJV)

---

### Hook
We treat life like a stadium.

A short window in the middle is "the game" — your twenties, thirties, forties.

Childhood is the warm-up. Old age is the cool-down.

And the parts before and after? We treat them like the stands. Just watching.

But God doesn't run a stadium.

In God's economy, there are no spectator seasons.

A child can serve Him. A teenager can serve Him. A grandparent can serve Him.

**Every decade has a divine assignment, and not one of them is wasted.**

---

### Big Idea
> Every season of life carries a God-given assignment — there is no wasted decade.

---

### Why This Matters
The enemy steals seasons by convincing you they don't count.

"You're too young to matter."

"You're too old to be used."

"This in-between season is just waiting."

All lies.

If God has given you breath in this season, He has given you an assignment in it. Longevity for God means serving Him faithfully through every chapter — not just the convenient ones.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Childhood Has an Assignment
The boy Samuel ministered before the Lord while he was still a child — and God spoke to him before he spoke to the high priest (1 Samuel 3:1–10).

A young heart is not too small for God to use.

"Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings hast thou ordained strength" (Psalm 8:2). A child's faith, worship, and obedience are not rehearsal — they are real service, received by God.

Childhood is the season to learn the voice of God early, so you recognize it for the rest of your life.

**Application:** If you have children near you, give them something real to do for God this week. If you remember your own childhood faith, thank God it counted.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Samuel 3:1–10; Psalm 8:2; Matthew 21:16

#### 2. Youth Has an Assignment
"Let no man despise thy youth; but be thou an example of the believers" (1 Timothy 4:12).

The young are not the church of tomorrow only — they are the church of today.

David faced Goliath as a youth. Daniel purposed in his heart as a young man in Babylon. Mary said "be it unto me according to thy word" while still young. God has always entrusted enormous assignments to the young.

"Remember now thy Creator in the days of thy youth" (Ecclesiastes 12:1) — because youth is the season to give God your strongest energy, not your leftovers.

**Application:** If you are young, offer God your strength now, not "someday." If you mentor the young, hand them a real assignment, not a waiting room.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Timothy 4:12; Ecclesiastes 12:1; 1 Samuel 17:32–37

#### 3. Adulthood Has an Assignment
The prime years bring the most strength, resources, and responsibility — and God expects fruit from them.

"Unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required" (Luke 12:48).

This is the season of building families, working with integrity, providing, leading, giving, and laboring in the harvest. Jesus carried out His entire public ministry in roughly three adult years and changed eternity.

The danger of adulthood is letting busyness crowd out the Master. Serve God *in* your work and family, not merely after them.

**Application:** Take one responsibility you already carry and consciously offer it to God as service today — your job, your home, your provision.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Luke 12:48; Colossians 3:23; 1 Corinthians 15:58

#### 4. Old Age Has an Assignment
"They shall still bring forth fruit in old age; they shall be fat and flourishing" (Psalm 92:14).

In God's kingdom, there is no retirement from fruitfulness.

Anna was a prophetess of about eighty-four who "departed not from the temple, but served God with fastings and prayers night and day," and was among the first to recognize the Christ-child (Luke 2:36–38). Simeon, old and waiting, held the salvation of the world in his arms.

The later seasons carry their own irreplaceable assignment: to pray, to bless, to testify, to pass faith to the next generation — fruit that only decades of walking with God can produce.

**Application:** If you are in a later season, ask God for your assignment now — to intercede, to mentor, to bless. If you know an older believer, ask them to pray over you this week.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 92:13–15; Luke 2:36–38; Titus 2:2–5

---

### Biblical Example
- **Samuel** served God as a child, as a young prophet, as a leader, and as an old man anointing kings — every season given to God (1 Samuel 3; 1 Samuel 12; 1 Samuel 16).
- **Moses** spent forty years a prince, forty years a shepherd, and forty years a deliverer — and God used all three.
- **Anna and Simeon** served faithfully into old age and were entrusted with seeing the Messiah (Luke 2:25–38).

From Samuel's first whispered "Speak; for thy servant heareth" as a child to his last act anointing David as an old man, no decade of his life was empty. Every season had an assignment, and he served God in each.

---

### Illustration
A fruit tree does not bear the same way all year — but it is never useless.

In spring it blossoms. In summer it grows and shelters. In autumn it gives its harvest. And even in winter, bare and quiet, its roots go deeper and it prepares the strength for the next harvest.

A foolish farmer might look at a winter tree and call it finished. A wise one knows the seasons are doing different work — and every one of them matters.

Your life has seasons too. Do not despise the one you're in.

**A modern picture:** Think of a long career where someone serves first as an apprentice, then a craftsman, then a foreman, then a mentor training the young. The role changes with each decade — but the work never stops being valuable. **God assigns you a role in every season of life; the title changes, but you are never benched.**

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Name the season you are in right now and ask: *"What is my God-given assignment here?"*
- Stop waiting for a "real" season — serve God in this one.
- Offer one current responsibility to God as worship today.
- Bless someone in a different season than yours — younger or older.

---

### Reflection Questions
- What season of life am I in — and have I accepted its assignment?
- Have I believed the lie that I'm too young, too old, or just waiting?
- Whom in another season could I serve or encourage this week?

### Longevity Insight
> Gerontologists have found that people who keep a sense of purpose live measurably longer and stay sharper than those who simply retire from meaning. Scripture said it long ago: the righteous "shall still bring forth fruit in old age." God has no spectator seasons and no surplus decades. The secret to a long, fruitful life is to keep showing up for your assignment — in every season, until the very last one.
