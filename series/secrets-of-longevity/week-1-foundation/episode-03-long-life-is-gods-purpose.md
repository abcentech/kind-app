# EPISODE 3
## Long Life Is God's Purpose

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 1: Foundation (*Life Begins with God*)

### Key Scripture
> "...the number of thy days I will fulfil."
> — Exodus 23:26 (KJV)

---

### Hook
God has never called anyone to retire from His purpose.

You can retire from a job.

You can step back from a title.

But there is no retirement from the assignment God gave your life.

As long as you have breath, you have a purpose to fulfill.

---

### Big Idea
> God preserves us not merely so we can live longer, but so we can finish our assignment.

---

### Why This Matters
God didn't promise to fill your *calendar*.

He promised to fulfill your *days*.

There is a difference between merely passing time and completing a God-given course.

Long life is not the goal. **Finishing well** is the goal.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Every Life Has an Assignment
You were sent, not just born. God has prepared works for you to walk in.

A long life is God giving you the *time* to complete the *task*.

**Application:** Identify what God has put in your hands to do — and refuse to coast.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Exodus 23:26; Ephesians 2:10; Acts 13:36

#### 2. God Numbers Our Days
Our days are not random. They are counted, ordered, and held by God.

When we understand that our days are numbered, we stop wasting them and start weighing
them.

**Application:** Ask God for a heart of wisdom to use your numbered days well.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 90:12; Psalm 139:16

#### 3. The Goal Is to Finish Well
Starting is common; finishing is rare.

God's desire is not merely that we begin the race, but that we cross the line still
carrying the faith.

**Application:** Live today as one stride in a race you intend to finish faithfully.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 2 Timothy 4:7; Acts 20:24

---

### Biblical Example
- **Jesus** finished His earthly ministry: "I have finished the work which thou gavest me
  to do" (John 17:4).
- **Paul** finished his course: "I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course"
  (2 Timothy 4:7).
- **Caleb** was still asking for mountains at eighty-five: "give me this mountain"
  (Joshua 14:10–12).

None of them merely survived to old age. They *completed* what God assigned.

---

### Illustration
A relay runner is not celebrated for starting fast.

He is celebrated for crossing the finish line **with the baton still in his hand.**

Plenty of runners explode off the blocks. The race is won by the one who carries the
baton all the way home.

The Christian life is the same. God's desire is not merely that we begin well — but that
we finish faithfully, still carrying what He entrusted to us.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Name the "baton" God has put in your hands — the assignment you must not drop.
- Refuse the lie that your useful years are behind you.
- Take one concrete step today that moves your God-given assignment forward.

---

### Reflection Questions
- What has God assigned me that I have not yet finished?
- Am I living to pass time, or to fulfill my days?

---

### Prayer

Teach me to number them, that I may live with wisdom.

Keep me faithful to the assignment You have given me.

Help me not only to live long, but to finish well — still carrying the faith.

### Longevity Insight
> Studies of healthy aging keep landing on one word: *purpose*. People with a clear
> reason to get up in the morning tend to live longer — and live better. God said it
> first. He doesn't just add days to your life; He gives your days something worth
> living for.
