# EPISODE 1
## Life Is Authored by God

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 1: Foundation (*Life Begins with God*)

### Key Scripture
> "And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils
> the breath of life; and man became a living soul."
> — Genesis 2:7 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Have you ever wondered where your very first breath came from?

You didn't ask for it.

You didn't earn it.

One moment there was nothing — and the next, there was *you*.

Before you had a name...

before anyone held you...

before your heart beat even once...

Life had to be *given*.

---

### Big Idea
> You are not an accident, and you are not self-made — life begins with the breath of God.

---

### Why This Matters
People spend years trying to discover the meaning of life while ignoring the One who gave
them life.

If God is the Author of life...

then He alone defines its purpose.

Before God gave Adam work, a wife, or a garden — He gave him *life*.

Every heartbeat is borrowed from God.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. God Is the Source of Life
Life did not begin in man. It began in God.

Everything that lives depends upon Him for every breath.

Science can describe life; it cannot create it from nothing. Only the Author can author.

**Application:** Begin today by acknowledging God as the Giver — not the universe, not
luck, not yourself.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Genesis 2:7; Acts 17:25; John 1:4

#### 2. Life Is Sacred
Because God created life, every human life has value — and that value never moves.

Age does not determine value.

Ability does not determine value.

Success does not determine value.

**God does.**

**Application:** Treat your own life, and every life around you, as something holy that
God Himself breathed into being.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Genesis 1:27; Psalm 139:13–14

#### 3. Life Is Given for a Purpose
God never creates without purpose. Every assignment begins with the gift of life.

You were not merely born — you were *sent*.

**Application:** Stop asking only "what do I want from life?" and start asking "what did
God give me life *for*?"

**Supporting Scriptures:** Ephesians 2:10; Jeremiah 29:11

---

### Biblical Example
- **Adam** received life directly from God's own breath (Genesis 2:7).
- **Jeremiah** was known by God before he was formed in the womb (Jeremiah 1:5).
- **John the Baptist** was filled with the Holy Ghost before he was born (Luke 1:15).
- **Jesus** came that we might have life, and have it more abundantly (John 10:10).

From the first man to the Son of God, the pattern is the same: life is God's to give, and
God gives it on purpose.

---

### Illustration
Imagine receiving a beautifully wrapped gift.

You admire the wrapping.

You display the box on a shelf.

But you never open it.

Many people admire life — they decorate it, photograph it, compare it — without ever
discovering *why* God gave it.

Life is God's gift. Purpose is what gives that gift meaning.

**A modern picture:** A smartphone may have the best design, the fastest processor, the
newest technology — but without power, it cannot fulfill a single purpose it was built
for. Our lives are the same. **God's breath is the power behind every life.**

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Thank God *specifically* for the gift of today — name it out loud.
- Live this day as borrowed time entrusted to you, not owned by you.
- Ask one question before every major decision today: *"Does this honor the One who gave
  me life?"*

---

### Reflection Questions
- Who gave me life?
- Am I living for the One who gave it?
- How can I honor God with the life He has entrusted to me?

---

Amen.

### Weekly Challenge
Every morning this week, pray:

*"Lord, thank You for the gift of today. Help me live this day for Your glory. May every
