# EPISODE 9
## Think Life

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 3: Focus (*Renewing the Mind*)

### Key Scripture
> "And be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind,
> that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God."
> — Romans 12:2 (KJV)

---

### Hook
What is the longest conversation you have ever had?

It isn't with your spouse.

It isn't with your closest friend.

It's the one happening inside your own head — right now — and it never stops.

You wake to it.

You fall asleep to it.

And that quiet, endless conversation is slowly shaping the kind of person you are becoming.

So here is the question that decides a life:

What are you telling yourself all day long?

---

### Big Idea
> A long, fruitful life is not built on what happens to you, but on the thoughts you allow
> to live in your mind.

---

### Why This Matters
Your thoughts today become your words tomorrow.

Your words become your actions.

Your actions become your habits — and your habits become your life.

Fear, worry, and bitterness do not just trouble the soul; they wear down the body and rob
the years. A mind set on death cannot produce a life of peace. But a mind renewed by God's
Word can carry you, strong and steady, into every decade He has given you.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. The Mind Must Be Renewed, Not Just Restrained
The world does not have to break into your house to capture you — it only has to capture
your mind.

Paul does not say *try harder* or *behave better*. He says **be transformed by the
renewing of your mind.** The Greek picture is of a continual, daily renewal — like a
garden that must be tended, not paved over once and forgotten.

You cannot simply empty your mind of bad thoughts. You must *fill* it with God's truth
until the old thinking has no room to live.

**Application:** Each morning, before the world speaks to you, let God speak first. Read one
verse and carry it through the day.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Romans 12:2; Ephesians 4:23; Colossians 3:2

#### 2. The Mind Set on Life Produces Peace
There are two ways to think, and they lead to two different places.

> "For to be carnally minded is death; but to be spiritually minded is life and peace."
> *(Romans 8:6)*

A mind chained to fear is already living a kind of death before the body ever fails. But a
mind anchored in God breathes life and peace into everything it touches.

Where you fix your mind, there you will live.

**Application:** When you catch yourself rehearsing the worst, stop and ask: *Is this
thought leading me toward life and peace, or toward fear and death?*

**Supporting Scriptures:** Romans 8:6; Isaiah 26:3; John 14:27

#### 3. Reject Fear — It Was Not Given by God
Fear is one of the loudest voices in the human mind, and it is a liar.

> "For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound
> mind." *(2 Timothy 1:7)*

Notice — a *sound mind* is a gift from God, not an accident of personality. Fear pretends
to protect you, but it shrinks your faith, steals your sleep, and shortens your peace.

You do not have to obey every thought that knocks. You get to choose which ones you let in.

**Application:** Name your fear out loud, then answer it out loud with a promise of God.
Don't argue with fear in silence — silence is where it grows.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 2 Timothy 1:7; Psalm 27:1; 1 John 4:18

#### 4. Think According to God's Word
A renewed mind is not an empty mind — it is a mind given the right things to think about.

> "Whatsoever things are true... honest... just... pure... lovely... of good report... think
> on these things." *(Philippians 4:8)*

God is not vague about what should fill your thoughts. He hands you a list. The believer
who keeps that list cannot be captured by despair, because there is no room left for it.

**Application:** When a dark thought comes, don't just push it away — replace it. Trade one
fearful thought for one true thing God has said.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Philippians 4:8; Joshua 1:8; Psalm 1:2–3

---

### Biblical Example
Consider **Caleb**.

Ten spies and Caleb saw the very same land — the same giants, the same walled cities, the
same impossible odds (Numbers 13). But ten of them *thought death*: "we be not able... we
were in our own sight as grasshoppers" (Numbers 13:31, 33).

Caleb thought *life*: "Let us go up at once, and possess it; for we are well able to
overcome it" (Numbers 13:30).

Same facts. Different minds. And it changed everything.

The ten died in the wilderness. Caleb, who fed his mind on God's promise, stood strong at
**eighty-five years old** and said, "as my strength was then, even so is my strength now"
(Joshua 14:11). The mind set on life carried him into a fruitful old age.

---

### Illustration
Think of a river.

A river does not choose its own course — it follows the channel that has been carved into
the ground. Year after year, the water deepens the path it already runs, until the river
cannot flow any other way.

Your thoughts are the same. Every time you rehearse a fear, you deepen the channel. Every
time you choose God's truth, you carve a new one.

You cannot stop the water from flowing — your mind will always think *something*. But by
God's Word, you can dig a new channel, and over time the river of your thinking will run
toward life instead of fear.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Catch the very next fearful or negative thought — and say it out loud so you can hear it.
- Answer it out loud with one verse of Scripture (start with 2 Timothy 1:7).
- Choose one true, lovely, good-report thing from Philippians 4:8 and dwell on it on
  purpose for sixty seconds.

---

### Reflection Questions
- What is the most common thought I rehearse when no one is watching?
- Is my mind set on life and peace, or on fear and death?
- What one verse could I carry into every hard moment this week?
