# EPISODE 10
## Speak Life

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 3: Focus (*Renewing the Mind*)

### Key Scripture
> "Death and life are in the power of the tongue: and they that love it shall eat the fruit
> thereof."
> — Proverbs 18:21 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Lean in close, because Solomon is about to hand you one of the most dangerous and most
beautiful truths in all of Scripture.

In your mouth — right now — you carry both death and life.

Not a little of each.

The full power of both.

A few words can crush a child for forty years.

A few words can lift a broken man off the floor.

You speak hundreds of times a day.

So the question is not *whether* you have this power —

it's *which one* you've been handing out.

---

### Big Idea
> Your words are seeds, and the life you live tomorrow is the harvest of what your mouth
> plants today.

---

### Why This Matters
You have been talking your whole life — but have you ever stopped to weigh what you've been
saying?

Many people pray for a blessed future while speaking defeat over their present. They ask
God for hope at church and rehearse despair at home.

Words set direction. The same tongue that can bless God can curse a brother; the same mouth
that can speak faith can plant fear. If you want to walk in life through every season, you
must learn to *speak* life — over yourself, your family, and your future.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Words Carry Real Power
God did not create the world with His hands. He created it with His *words*: "And God said,
Let there be light: and there was light" (Genesis 1:3).

You are made in His image — and your words carry weight too.

> "Death and life are in the power of the tongue." *(Proverbs 18:21)*

Your tongue is not just describing your life. It is *helping to build it*. Words are not
empty air; they are seeds that take root.

**Application:** Before you speak today, ask one question: *Am I about to plant life or
plant death?*

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 18:21; Genesis 1:3; Proverbs 12:18

#### 2. You Will Eat the Fruit of Your Words
Solomon adds a sobering line: "they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof."

> "A man's belly shall be satisfied with the fruit of his mouth." *(Proverbs 18:20)*

What you sow with your mouth, you will eventually have to swallow. Speak bitterness, and
you'll live in a bitter house. Speak faith, and you'll dwell in a home of hope.

You are not only the speaker of your words — you are the one who has to live in them.

**Application:** Notice the home your words are building. Would you want to live in the
house your last ten sentences just framed?

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 18:20; Galatians 6:7; Matthew 12:37

#### 3. Speak Faith, Not Fear
Faith is not a feeling you wait for — often it is a word you choose to speak first.

> "I believed, therefore have I spoken." *(Psalm 116:10; 2 Corinthians 4:13)*

When the report is bad and the body is weak, the believer does not deny the trouble — but he
refuses to let the trouble have the last word. He answers fear with what God has said.

David did not whisper to Goliath about how big the giant was. He spoke faith out loud
before he ever swung the sling (1 Samuel 17:45–46).

**Application:** When fear gives you a sentence to repeat, refuse it — and say a promise of
God out loud instead.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 2 Corinthians 4:13; 1 Samuel 17:45–46; Mark 11:23

#### 4. Speak Life Over Others
The tongue is small, but James says it steers the whole course of a life like a rudder
steers a great ship (James 3:4–5).

> "Let no corrupt communication proceed out of your mouth, but that which is good to the use
> of edifying... that it may minister grace unto the hearers." *(Ephesians 4:29)*

Every person you speak to today is being either built up or torn down by your words. You can
be the one who plants courage in a discouraged heart with a single sentence.

**Application:** Speak one specific, true word of blessing over someone today — your child,
your spouse, a coworker, a stranger.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Ephesians 4:29; James 3:4–6; Proverbs 16:24

---

### Biblical Example
Watch **the twelve spies** again — but this time, listen to their *mouths* (Numbers 13–14).

Ten of them came back and *spoke death*: "we be not able... it is a land that eateth up the
inhabitants" (Numbers 13:31–32). Their report spread through the whole camp until two
million people wept all night.

Then God said something terrifying: "As ye have spoken in mine ears, so will I do to you"
(Numbers 14:28). They had said they would die in the wilderness — and they did.

But **Caleb and Joshua** spoke life: "the LORD is with us: fear them not" (Numbers 14:9).
And those two — the ones who spoke life — were the only ones of that generation to walk into
the Promised Land.

Their words framed their future. So will yours.

---

### Illustration
A farmer never stands at the edge of an empty field shouting at it to produce wheat.

He knows the field can only give back what he first puts in. So he plants — carefully,
patiently, seed by seed — and then he waits for the harvest the seeds were always going to
bring.

Your mouth is sowing into the field of your life every single day. You cannot plant complaint
and harvest peace. You cannot plant fear and harvest faith. You cannot plant cursing and
harvest blessing.

If you don't like the harvest you're living in, change the seed in your mouth.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Catch one negative or fearful word before it leaves your mouth — and replace it out loud
  with what God's Word says.
- Speak one specific blessing over yourself this morning from Scripture.
- Say one true word of life over another person before the day ends.

---

### Reflection Questions
- If my words became my future, what kind of life am I building?
- Whose voice in my past spoke life or death over me — and which do I now repeat?
- What is one sentence I keep saying that I need to stop planting?

### Longevity Insight
> Researchers have found that a hopeful, encouraging outlook — spoken and practiced — is
> linked to longer, healthier living, while a steady diet of negative speech feeds stress
> and decline. Solomon wrote the headline three thousand years ago: *death and life are in
> the power of the tongue.* The mouth that speaks life is also helping the body live it.
