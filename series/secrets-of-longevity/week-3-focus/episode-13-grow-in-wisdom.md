# EPISODE 13
## Grow in Wisdom

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 3: Focus (*Renewing the Mind*)

### Key Scripture
> "Length of days is in her right hand; and in her left hand riches and honour."
> — Proverbs 3:16 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Here is a riddle worth more than gold.

A man can have a head full of facts and still ruin his life.

Another can have far less information and live full of peace, fruitful to the end.

What's the difference between them?

It isn't *what they know*.

It's something deeper — something the Bible says you can ask God for, and He will give it.

Solomon called it the principal thing.

And he said it carries *length of days* in its very right hand.

---

### Big Idea
> Knowledge fills your head, understanding opens your eyes, but wisdom directs your steps —
> and wisdom multiplies your fruitful years.

---

### Why This Matters
We live in an age drowning in information and starving for wisdom. Never have people known so
much and lived so foolishly.

A renewed mind is not just a *full* mind — it is a *wise* one. Wisdom is the skill of living
life God's way, and it is the crown of everything Week 3 has been building toward: think
life, speak life, choose joy, keep learning — and let it all become wisdom.

Solomon says wisdom holds *length of days* in her right hand. This is where renewing the mind
and a long, fruitful life finally meet.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Know the Difference: Knowledge, Understanding, Wisdom
These three words are often used as if they mean the same thing. They do not.

> "The LORD by wisdom hath founded the earth; by understanding hath he established the
> heavens. By his knowledge the depths are broken up." *(Proverbs 3:19–20)*

**Knowledge** is the gathering of facts — it *informs* you.
**Understanding** is the grasp of how those facts fit together — it *explains* them.
**Wisdom** is the skill to do the right thing with what you know — it *applies* them.

You can have knowledge without wisdom. But you cannot have wisdom without using what you
know rightly. Wisdom is knowledge on its feet, walking in the fear of the Lord.

**Application:** Don't stop at knowing the right thing — ask God how to *do* the right thing,
today.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 3:19–20; Proverbs 4:7; Proverbs 9:10

#### 2. Wisdom Begins with the Fear of the Lord
You cannot get wisdom by being clever. It starts on your knees.

> "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom." *(Proverbs 9:10)*

To fear the Lord is to honor Him as God — to trust His way above your own. That is why
Proverbs 3 says, "Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own
understanding" (Proverbs 3:5). The wisest thing a mind can do is bow to the One who made it.

**Application:** In your next decision, lean not on your own understanding — pause and ask God
first.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 9:10; Proverbs 3:5–7; Job 28:28

#### 3. Ask God for Wisdom — He Gives It Freely
You do not have to be born wise. Wisdom is a gift God delights to give to anyone who asks.

> "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and
> upbraideth not; and it shall be given him." *(James 1:5)*

God does not roll His eyes at the one who asks. He gives generously, without scolding. Solomon
asked for wisdom instead of riches or long life — and God gave him all three (1 Kings 3:9–13).

**Application:** Ask God plainly for wisdom this morning, and expect Him to give it.

**Supporting Scriptures:** James 1:5; 1 Kings 3:9–13; Proverbs 2:6

#### 4. Wisdom Multiplies Your Fruitful Years
Here is the longevity promise at the heart of Proverbs 3.

> "Length of days is in her right hand; and in her left hand riches and honour... She is a
> tree of life to them that lay hold upon her." *(Proverbs 3:16, 18)*

Wisdom is not only spiritual; it is intensely practical. The wise avoid the ruin that
foolishness brings — the broken health, the broken homes, the wasted decades. Living God's way
protects and lengthens a life, and fills the years with fruit. Wisdom is a *tree of life* to
all who take hold of her.

**Application:** Choose the wise path today even when the foolish one is faster — and guard
the years God has given you.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 3:16–18; Proverbs 3:1–2; Proverbs 10:27

---

### Biblical Example
**Solomon** stood at the start of his reign and God offered him anything (1 Kings 3:5).

He could have asked for riches. He could have asked for victory over his enemies. He could
have asked, plainly, for long life.

Instead he asked for "an understanding heart to judge thy people, that I may discern between
good and bad" (1 Kings 3:9). He asked for *wisdom*.

And God answered: "Because thou hast asked this thing... behold, I have done according to thy
words... And I have also given thee that which thou hast not asked, both riches, and honour...
And if thou wilt walk in my ways... then I will lengthen thy days" (1 Kings 3:11–14).

Solomon reached for wisdom — and length of days came along in her right hand, exactly as
Proverbs promised.

But there is a warning in his story too: in his later years Solomon stopped walking in the
very wisdom he was given, and it cost him dearly (1 Kings 11). Wisdom must not only be
*received* — it must be *kept*.

---

### Illustration
Picture three people standing before a great mountain river they must cross.

The **first** has *knowledge*: he has read about rivers, currents, and crossings. He knows the
facts.

The **second** has *understanding*: he looks at this particular river and reads it — he sees
where the current runs fast, where the rocks lie hidden, where the water is deep.

The **third** has *wisdom*: she takes what is known and understood, and actually crosses
safely — choosing the right place, the right step, the right moment.

Many people drown in rivers they could describe perfectly. Knowledge and understanding can
tell you about the water. Only wisdom gets you to the other side — and keeps you alive to
cross again tomorrow.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Ask God plainly for wisdom this morning, trusting James 1:5 that He gives it freely.
- In your next decision, lean not on your own understanding — pause and ask, *"Lord, what is
  the wise thing to do?"*
- Take one fearful or negative thought and replace it out loud with what God's Word says,
  letting wisdom — not fear — direct your step.

---

### Reflection Questions
- Am I gathering knowledge but failing to live wisely?
- Where am I leaning on my own understanding instead of trusting the Lord?
- What is one foolish path I keep choosing because it's faster?

### Longevity Insight
> Wise living quietly lengthens life: the wise avoid ruinous choices, keep their peace, and
> guard their health — while folly burns through the years. Christ Himself "increased in
> wisdom and stature, and in favour with God and man" (Luke 2:52). And He is "made unto us
> wisdom" (1 Corinthians 1:30). To grow in wisdom is to grow in Christ — and Proverbs
> promises that *length of days is in her right hand.*
