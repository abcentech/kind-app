# EPISODE 12
## Keep Learning

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 3: Focus (*Renewing the Mind*)

### Key Scripture
> "A wise man will hear, and will increase learning; and a man of understanding shall attain
> unto wise counsels."
> — Proverbs 1:5 (KJV)

---

### Hook
When do you think a person stops growing?

Most people answer with an age.

But here is something strange.

The body stops growing taller in our teens.

The mind, though — the mind can keep growing until your very last day on earth.

So why do so many people stop?

Why do we treat learning like school — something you finish, graduate from, and leave
behind?

The wisest man in the Bible never graduated.

And neither should you.

---

### Big Idea
> A heart that keeps learning never grows old, because every decade can know more of God
> than the one before it.

---

### Why This Matters
There is a quiet danger that comes with age and experience: we begin to think we already
know.

But the moment you stop learning is the moment you start shrinking. A closed mind grows
stale; a teachable heart stays alive.

God designed you to grow all the way home. Your eyes may dim and your steps may slow, but
your knowledge of God can deepen every single year. The believer who keeps learning keeps
bearing fruit — and a fruitful mind helps make for a fruitful life.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. The Wise Keep Learning — Fools Think They Know
The very first chapter of Proverbs draws the dividing line.

> "A wise man will hear, and will increase learning... but fools despise wisdom and
> instruction." *(Proverbs 1:5, 7)*

The mark of a wise person is not how much they already know — it is their hunger to keep
learning. The fool stops listening because he thinks he has arrived. The wise man leans in,
because he knows he never has.

**Application:** Stay teachable on purpose. Ask one honest question today instead of giving
one more answer.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 1:5; Proverbs 9:9; Proverbs 12:1

#### 2. Keep Learning the Word of God Above All
Not all learning is equal. A man can fill his head with facts and still die a fool. The
learning that gives life is the knowledge of God.

> "But grow in grace, and in the knowledge of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ."
> *(2 Peter 3:18)*

The Bible is not a book you read once and shelve. It is a well that never runs dry — you can
return to the same verse for fifty years and still draw out something new. Every decade
should know more of God than the last.

**Application:** Don't just *read* a verse today — *study* one. Ask what it reveals about
God you didn't see before.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 2 Peter 3:18; Psalm 119:97–99; 2 Timothy 2:15

#### 3. Learning Keeps the Mind Sharp and the Life Fruitful
A renewed mind is an *active* mind. God uses what you keep learning to bear fruit in every
season — even the late ones.

> "Those that be planted in the house of the LORD shall flourish in the courts of our God.
> They shall still bring forth fruit in old age." *(Psalm 92:13–14)*

The tree that keeps drawing water keeps producing fruit. The believer who keeps drawing from
God's wisdom keeps producing too — counsel, encouragement, and faith — long after others
have coasted to a stop.

**Application:** Pick up something worth learning — a book of the Bible, a skill, a subject
that helps you serve — and begin it this week.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 92:13–14; Proverbs 4:18; Hosea 4:6

#### 4. The Humble Keep Learning All Their Lives
Learning requires humility — and humility is what keeps a teachable heart open to the end.

> "Receive my instruction, and not silver; and knowledge rather than choice gold."
> *(Proverbs 8:10)*

Even Apollos — "an eloquent man, and mighty in the scriptures" — sat down and let Aquila and
Priscilla teach him "the way of God more perfectly" (Acts 18:24–26). A gifted man stayed
teachable, and God used him even more.

**Application:** Let someone teach you something this week — and receive it with gladness,
not pride.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Acts 18:24–26; Proverbs 8:10; James 1:21

---

### Biblical Example
Look at how God used men who never stopped learning:

- **Moses** spent his first forty years learning in Pharaoh's palace, his next forty learning
  in the wilderness, and only *then*, at eighty, was he ready to be used (Acts 7:22–23, 30).
  He was still learning from God on the mountain at the end of his life (Exodus 33:13).
- **Daniel** was taught the learning of Babylon as a youth (Daniel 1:4), yet he was still
  receiving fresh revelation as an old man in his eighties (Daniel 10).
- **Paul**, near the end of his life and in prison, asked Timothy to bring him "the books,
  but especially the parchments" (2 Timothy 4:13). The greatest theologian of the church was
  still studying when the executioner was near.
- **Apollos** was already mighty in the Scriptures, yet humbly learned the way of God more
  perfectly (Acts 18:26).

From the palace to the prison, the pattern holds: God's most fruitful servants were lifelong
learners.

---

### Illustration
Think of a tree and a fence post.

Both start as wood. But a fence post is *finished* wood — it was cut down, dried out, and set
in the ground. It will never grow another inch. It can only weather, crack, and rot, year by
year, until one day it has to be replaced.

A tree is *living* wood. It keeps drinking, keeps reaching, keeps adding new rings — one for
every year it lives. The oldest trees on earth are still growing.

A mind that stops learning becomes a fence post — fixed, drying, slowly decaying. A mind that
keeps learning stays a tree — alive, fruitful, adding a new ring with every decade.

Which kind of wood will you be in your seventies?

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Ask one honest question today instead of assuming you already know the answer.
- Study one verse — don't just read it — and write down one new thing it shows you about God.
- Begin learning one worthwhile thing this week, and let someone else teach you part of it.

### Longevity Insight
> Researchers studying healthy aging keep finding the same thing: minds that stay curious and
> keep learning stay sharper and steadier into old age, while idle minds decline faster.
> Scripture pointed there first — the wise *increase learning*, and the righteous "still
> bring forth fruit in old age." A learning mind is a living mind, and a living mind helps
> sustain a long, fruitful life.
