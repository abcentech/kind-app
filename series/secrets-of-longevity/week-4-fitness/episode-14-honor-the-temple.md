# EPISODE 14
## Honor the Temple

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 4: Fitness (*Stewarding the Body*)

### Key Scripture
> "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which
> ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore
> glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God's."
> — 1 Corinthians 6:19–20 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Imagine you were handed the keys to a building.

Not just any building —

a temple.

Holy ground.

A place where God Himself chose to dwell.

Now imagine you let it crumble.

You never cleaned it.

You filled it with junk.

You ran it into the ground because you thought it was yours to ruin.

Here is the truth most people never hear:

**you are already holding those keys.**

The temple is your body.

---

### Big Idea
> Your body is not your own — it is God's temple, and stewarding it well is an act of worship.

---

### Why This Matters
We live in a world that treats the body in two extremes.

Some worship it — chasing vanity, mirrors, and applause.

Others neglect it — abusing it as if it doesn't matter.

But God offers a third way.

The body is not a god to be worshipped, and not a thing to be wasted.

It is a temple to be *honored* — for the glory of the One who lives in it.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Your Body Belongs to God
Under the old covenant, God dwelt in a temple of stone.

Now, by His Spirit, He dwells in something far more astonishing — *you*.

That changes everything about how you treat the house He lives in.

You did not make your body. You cannot add one cubit to your stature by worrying
(Matthew 6:27). It was given, it was bought, and it is held in trust.

A steward does not own the estate. He manages it for the Master.

**Application:** Today, surrender ownership. Pray, "Lord, this body is Yours — show me how
You want it cared for."

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Corinthians 6:19–20; Psalm 100:3; 1 Corinthians 3:16

#### 2. Honor Begins with Reverence, Not Vanity
The temple in Jerusalem was not honored because it was beautiful to look at.

It was honored because *God was there*.

So it is with your body.

We do not steward it to be admired, or to chase an image the world keeps changing.

We steward it because the Holy Ghost dwells within.

Vanity asks, "How do I look?" Stewardship asks, "How do I serve?"

**Application:** Examine your motives. Care for your body as worship to God, not as a
performance for people.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Samuel 16:7; Proverbs 31:30; Romans 12:1

#### 3. You Were Bought with a Price
"Ye are not your own; for ye are bought with a price."

That price was the blood of Jesus Christ.

The cross is the reason your body is sacred.

He purchased not only your soul but your whole self — body, mind, and spirit.

To neglect the body He died to redeem is to forget what it cost Him.

**Application:** Let the cross set your standard. If Christ valued your body enough to
redeem it, you can value it enough to care for it.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Corinthians 6:20; 1 Peter 1:18–19; Acts 20:28

#### 4. Stewardship Is Worship, and Worship Is Daily
Paul says, "glorify God in your body."

Not once. Not on Sundays. *In your body* — in the ordinary acts of every day.

How you eat, rest, move, and keep yourself pure is not separate from your worship.

It *is* worship — your reasonable service (Romans 12:1).

**Application:** Choose one ordinary act today — a meal, a walk, an early night — and offer
it to God on purpose as worship.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Romans 12:1; Colossians 3:17; 1 Corinthians 10:31

---

### Biblical Example
- **Daniel** purposed in his heart not to defile himself, honoring God with what he put
  into his body even in a pagan palace (Daniel 1:8).
- **Solomon** built the temple with overwhelming care, sparing no excellence because of
  Whose house it was (1 Kings 6).
- **Jesus** drove out those who defiled the temple, declaring, "My house shall be called
  the house of prayer" (Matthew 21:13) — zeal for His Father's dwelling.
- **Paul**, though imprisoned and beaten, kept his body under subjection so it would serve
  the gospel and not master him (1 Corinthians 9:27).

The pattern is clear: when God dwells somewhere, that place is treated with honor.

---

### Illustration
Think of a master craftsman who builds a violin by hand.

He selects the wood for years. He shapes every curve. He tunes every string.

When it is finished, he does not toss it in a corner or leave it in the rain.

He keeps it in a case. He cleans it. He plays it with care —

because the maker's whole heart went into it, and it was built to make music.

Your body is God's handiwork, fearfully and wonderfully made (Psalm 139:14).

He did not form you to be neglected.

He formed you to make a kind of music with your life —

a life that glorifies Him.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Thank God that your body is His temple, not your possession.
- Identify one way you have been neglecting or idolizing your body — and repent of it.
- Offer one concrete act of care today as worship: a glass of water, a walk, an early night.
- Before a choice about food, rest, or habit, ask: *"Does this honor the One who lives in me?"*

---

### Reflection Questions
- Do I treat my body as my own, or as God's temple?
- Is my care for my body driven by worship or by vanity?
- What would change if I remembered the price that was paid for me?

---

### Prayer

Teach me to honor my body — not for vanity, but for Your glory.

Let everything I do with this body bring honor to the One who bought me with a price.

---

### Weekly Challenge
This week, honor God with your body in one concrete way each day — a healthier meal, a
walk, earlier sleep, or a fast — as worship, not vanity.

---

### Longevity Insight
> The buildings that last for centuries are the ones that are maintained — cleaned,
> repaired, and treated as valuable. Neglect is the silent destroyer; it asks nothing of
> you today and takes everything tomorrow. Your body is the same. To steward it now, as
> God's temple, is to invest in a life that can serve Him for many decades. You are not
> maintaining a possession — you are honoring a place where God lives.
