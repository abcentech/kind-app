# EPISODE 18
## Cleanse for Life

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 4: Fitness (*Stewarding the Body*)

### Key Scripture
> "Having therefore these promises, dearly beloved, let us cleanse ourselves from all
> filthiness of the flesh and spirit, perfecting holiness in the fear of God."
> — 2 Corinthians 7:1 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Picture a clear mountain spring.

Cold. Pure. Life-giving.

Now imagine someone, day after day, tossing in a little poison.

Just a little.

A small habit here.

A toxin there.

Nothing dramatic — no one would notice for a while.

But over time the spring grows cloudy.

Then bitter.

Then it can no longer give the life it was made to give.

That is what unclean habits do to the temple of God.

Slowly. Quietly. One small compromise at a time.

But there is good news:

what is defiled can be cleansed.

---

### Big Idea
> Stewarding the body means keeping it clean — in purity, temperance, and self-control — for the glory of God.

---

### Why This Matters
The things that shorten a life are rarely a single dramatic event.

They are habits.

What we keep putting in. What we refuse to put down.

The slow poisons — of the body and of the soul — that we tolerate because they feel small.

But God calls His temple to be clean.

Not perfect by our striving —

but cleansed, guarded, and kept pure for His use.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. God Calls Us to Cleanse Body and Spirit
Paul says, "cleanse ourselves from all filthiness of the flesh and spirit" (2 Corinthians 7:1).

Notice — *both*. Body and spirit.

The two are not separate. A habit that poisons the body often poisons the soul, and a
defiled heart soon defiles the body.

To steward the temple is to keep both clean before God.

**Application:** Ask God to show you one "filthiness" — a habit of body or spirit — and let
Him cleanse it.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 2 Corinthians 7:1; Psalm 51:10; 1 John 1:9

#### 2. Temperance Is the Fruit of the Spirit
Self-control is not human willpower; it is fruit the Spirit grows.

"The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace... temperance: against such there is no law"
(Galatians 5:22–23).

Temperance is the power to say no to the flesh and yes to God —

over food, drink, appetite, and every habit that wants to master us.

You don't manufacture it. You yield to the Spirit, and He produces it.

**Application:** Where you've been ruled by a craving, stop striving alone — ask the Holy
Spirit to grow self-control in you.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Galatians 5:22–23; Titus 2:11–12; 2 Peter 1:5–6

#### 3. Flee What Defiles
Some things are not to be managed — they are to be fled.

"Flee fornication" (1 Corinthians 6:18). "Abstain from fleshly lusts, which war against the
soul" (1 Peter 2:11).

The drunkard, the addict, the impure — Scripture warns plainly that these defile the temple
and shorten the life God gave (Proverbs 23:29–32).

Purity is not prudishness. It is protecting what God paid for.

**Application:** Identify one thing that defiles you and *flee* it — remove it, don't
negotiate with it.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Corinthians 6:18; 1 Peter 2:11; Proverbs 23:29–32

#### 4. Fasting Cleanses and Refocuses
Fasting is one of God's oldest tools for cleansing.

It says no to the body for a season so the spirit can say a deeper yes to God.

It humbles the flesh, sharpens prayer, and breaks the grip of appetite.

"Is not this the fast that I have chosen?... to loose the bands of wickedness" (Isaiah 58:6).

Jesus assumed His followers would fast: "when ye fast" — not *if* (Matthew 6:16).

**Application:** Set aside one meal, or one day, to fast and pray this week — let the body
serve the spirit.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Isaiah 58:6; Matthew 6:16–18; Joel 2:12

---

### Biblical Example
Consider **Joseph** in Potiphar's house.

Day after day he was tempted to defile himself.

Potiphar's wife pressed him again and again. The opportunity was constant; the secrecy
seemed safe.

But Joseph refused: "How then can I do this great wickedness, and sin against God?"
(Genesis 39:9).

And when she seized him, the Bible says he "left his garment in her hand, and fled, and got
him out" (Genesis 39:12).

He did not stay to reason with temptation. He *fled*.

Joseph kept himself clean — and God lifted him to preserve nations and live a long,
fruitful life, dying at 110 (Genesis 50:26).

Purity guarded his future.

---

### Illustration
Think of how a hospital guards a sterile room.

Before surgery, everything is scrubbed. Hands are washed again and again. Nothing unclean
is allowed in, because even the smallest contamination can cost a life.

Why such care?

Because something precious is at stake — a human life on the table.

Your body is a far more sacred room.

The Holy Ghost dwells there.

It is worth guarding with the same vigilance — keeping out the toxins, the habits, the
impurities that would defile the place where God lives.

Cleanse the room. Guard the door. Keep it holy.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Ask God to reveal one "filthiness of flesh or spirit" — and confess it to Him today.
- Flee one thing that defiles you; remove it rather than manage it.
- Plan a fast this week — one meal or one day — to humble the body and seek God.
- Pray for the Spirit's fruit of temperance over your strongest craving.

---

### Reflection Questions
- What small "poisons" have I been tolerating in God's temple?
- Where do I need to flee instead of negotiate?
- Am I trusting willpower, or the Spirit, to make me temperate?

---

### Longevity Insight
> The habits that most quietly steal years — tobacco, drunkenness, impurity, the slow drip
> of toxins we tolerate — almost never feel urgent in the moment. That is exactly why they
> are so dangerous. Yet the body is remarkably forgiving: when the poison stops, healing
> begins, often within days. Scripture and science agree — cleansing the temple, and
> keeping it clean, adds life. "Let us cleanse ourselves from all filthiness of the flesh
> and spirit" (2 Corinthians 7:1). What is defiled can be cleansed — and a cleansed temple
> serves God longer.
