# EPISODE 17
## Move for Life

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 4: Fitness (*Stewarding the Body*)

### Key Scripture
> "And the LORD God took the man, and put him into the garden of Eden to dress it and to
> keep it."
> — Genesis 2:15 (KJV)

---

### Hook
Before there was sin...

before there was sorrow...

before there was even a single tear in all the world —

there was work.

God placed the first man in a garden and gave him something to do:

"to dress it and to keep it."

Adam was made to move.

To tend. To labor. To use his hands and his strength.

We often imagine paradise as endless lounging.

But God's first gift to man, after life and breath, was *purposeful activity*.

Your body was not designed to sit still.

It was designed to move.

---

### Big Idea
> God designed your body to move and work, and keeping it active is part of stewarding it for His service.

---

### Why This Matters
Our world has made stillness easy.

Screens. Chairs. Cars. Convenience at every turn.

We can now go days barely moving our bodies at all.

But a body built for the garden does not thrive in a chair.

Strength fades when it is never used.

And a weakened body cannot serve God, family, and neighbor the way it was made to.

Movement is not vanity. It is readiness — keeping the temple fit for its purpose.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Work and Movement Were God's Original Design
Before the fall, God gave Adam a garden to tend.

Work is not a curse; the *toil* of work came with the fall, but the work itself was a gift
(Genesis 2:15).

God made the body with muscles, lungs, and a heart that grow stronger when used.

To move and labor is to live the way you were designed to live.

**Application:** See your daily activity — work, chores, walking — as part of God's good
design, not a burden to escape.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Genesis 2:15; Genesis 3:19; Ecclesiastes 9:10

#### 2. The Body Grows Strong Through Use
Scripture honors strength as a gift to be stewarded, not flaunted.

"She girdeth her loins with strength, and strengtheneth her arms" — the worthy woman of
Proverbs is physically capable for her work (Proverbs 31:17).

A body kept active stays ready. A body never used grows weak.

This is not about appearance — it is about being fit to do what God calls you to do.

**Application:** Move your body today — a walk, lifting, stretching, honest labor. Keep the
temple strong for service.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 31:17; 1 Corinthians 9:27; Proverbs 24:30–34

#### 3. Bodily Exercise Profits — In Proportion
Paul gives the balance every believer needs:

"For bodily exercise profiteth little: but godliness is profitable unto all things, having
promise of the life that now is, and of that which is to come" (1 Timothy 4:8).

Notice — bodily exercise *profits*. It is good.

But it is "little" compared to godliness.

We keep the body fit so it can serve a higher purpose, never as the purpose itself.

Movement serves godliness; it never replaces it.

**Application:** Care for your body, but keep it in its place — under the priority of
knowing and serving God.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Timothy 4:8; Colossians 3:23; 1 Corinthians 6:13

#### 4. Diligence Honors God; Slothfulness Wastes the Temple
Scripture has stern words for the sluggard who lets his body and his fields go to ruin
(Proverbs 24:30–34).

Laziness is not rest — it is the neglect of a gift.

The diligent steward keeps moving, keeps tending, keeps the body ready for the Master's
use.

Movement is one practical way we refuse slothfulness and stay fruitful.

**Application:** Refuse the chair as a default. Choose one small act of diligence and motion
today.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 24:30–34; Proverbs 6:6–11; Colossians 3:23

---

### Biblical Example
Consider **Caleb** at eighty-five years old.

Forty-five years after spying out the land, he stood before Joshua and declared:

"As yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day that Moses sent me: as my strength was
then, even so is my strength now, for war, both to go out, and to come in" (Joshua 14:11).

And what did he ask for? Not retirement. Not ease.

"Give me this mountain" (Joshua 14:12).

Caleb kept his body strong and ready for decades —

and at an age when most men sit down, he was still climbing mountains for God.

A long life lived for God is often a *moving* life.

---

### Illustration
Think of a door hinge.

A hinge that is used every day — swinging open and shut — stays smooth and strong.

But a hinge that is never moved?

It rusts.

It stiffens.

It seizes up until it can no longer do the one thing it was made to do.

There is an old truth in it: *what is not used is lost.*

The body God gave you works the same way.

Movement keeps it free, strong, and ready.

Stillness, over time, lets it rust.

Keep the hinge swinging.

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Take a walk today — even ten minutes — and use it to pray and thank God for your body.
- Choose the active option once: the stairs, the longer route, the standing task.
- Do one honest piece of physical work as worship, "as to the Lord" (Colossians 3:23).
- Keep it in proportion: care for the body, but feed your soul first.

---

### Reflection Questions
- Am I keeping my body ready for the work God has for me?
- Have I let convenience make me weak?
- Is my care for my body in right proportion to my care for my soul?

---

And keep my heart in proportion: my body fit, but my soul fed first.

### Weekly Challenge
This week, honor God with your body in one concrete way each day — a healthier meal, a

---

### Longevity Insight
> Researchers studying the longest-lived people on earth found that almost none of them
> "exercise" the way we picture it — no gyms, no machines. Instead, movement is woven into
> their whole day: they walk, they garden, they work with their hands, they keep moving
> into their nineties. It is remarkably close to Eden — a man in a garden "to dress it and
> to keep it" (Genesis 2:15). You were not built for the chair. Keep moving, keep tending,
> and keep your body ready for a long life of service.
