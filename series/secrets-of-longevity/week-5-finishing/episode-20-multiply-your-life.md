# EPISODE 20
## Multiply Your Life

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 5: Finishing (*Leaving a Legacy*)

### Key Scripture
> "And the things that thou hast heard of me among many witnesses, the same commit thou to
> faithful men, who shall be able to teach others also."
> — 2 Timothy 2:2 (KJV)

---

### Hook
There is a kind of life that ends when you do.

And there is a kind of life that keeps going long after your funeral.

The difference is not how famous you were.

It is not how much you accumulated.

It is whether you poured what God gave you into someone else —

someone who will pour it into someone else —

after you are gone.

The longest life is not the one with the most years.

It is the one that **multiplies.**

---

### Big Idea
> You will not live forever in your body, so pour your life into people who will carry it
> after you.

---

### Why This Matters
Most of what you own will be sold, spent, or thrown away within a generation.

But what you *deposit* in a person can travel through centuries.

Jesus did not write a book. He did not build a building. He invested His three years in
twelve men — and that investment is still spreading across the earth today.

If you want a life that outlives you, stop trying to be irreplaceable. Start trying to be
*reproducible.*

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Spiritual Multiplication Is God's Strategy
Look closely at 2 Timothy 2:2 and you will count four generations in a single verse: Paul
→ Timothy → faithful men → others also.

That is not addition. That is *multiplication.*

God's plan for filling the earth with His glory has never been one great voice; it has
always been faithful people reproducing faithful people.

A teacher who only gathers a crowd leaves a crowd. A teacher who *makes disciples* leaves
an army that outlasts him.

**Application:** Stop measuring your impact only by what you can do yourself. Start asking,
*"Who am I raising up to do this when I no longer can?"*

**Supporting Scriptures:** 2 Timothy 2:2; Matthew 28:19–20; 2 Timothy 1:5

#### 2. Invest in the Next Generation on Purpose
Multiplication does not happen by accident. Nobody drifts into discipleship.

The next generation is always one decision away from forgetting God — Scripture warns of a
generation that "knew not the LORD" because the one before it failed to pass the faith on
(Judges 2:10).

You are a bridge. The faith reached you because someone carried it across to you. Now you
stand between what you received and a generation that has not yet received it.

**Application:** Choose one younger person — a child, a grandchild, a new believer, a
junior at work — and decide, on purpose, to pour into them what God has poured into you.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Deuteronomy 6:6–7; Psalm 78:4–7; Judges 2:10

#### 3. Leave Spiritual Sons and Daughters, Not Just Heirs
Paul had no biological children, yet he was a father many times over. He called Timothy
"my own son in the faith" (1 Timothy 1:2) and Titus "mine own son after the common faith"
(Titus 1:4).

A legacy of property divides as it passes down. A legacy of faith *multiplies* as it
passes down.

You may leave money to your children. But the greatest inheritance you can leave anyone is
a living faith and a finished example.

**Application:** Think of your legacy in terms of *people you formed*, not just things you
leave. Whose faith will be stronger because you lived?

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Timothy 1:2; Titus 1:4; 3 John 1:4

#### 4. Multiplication Costs You — and That's the Point
Pouring into others is not convenient. It costs time, patience, and the humility to be
surpassed by those you train.

Elijah threw his mantle on Elisha while Elisha was plowing — and Elisha left everything to
follow and *serve* him (1 Kings 19:19–21). The mentor must give; the student must learn to
pour water on a master's hands before he ever does miracles.

But the day came when Elisha asked for a double portion of Elijah's spirit — and got it
(2 Kings 2:9). The student went further than the teacher. **That is success, not
failure.**

**Application:** Be willing to be inconvenienced for someone's growth. And rejoice when
they go further than you did — that is multiplication working as God designed it.

**Supporting Scriptures:** 1 Kings 19:19–21; 2 Kings 2:9–15; John 14:12

---

### Biblical Example
- **Paul and Timothy** — Paul found a young disciple at Lystra (Acts 16:1–3), took him as a
  son, trained him, and handed him a ministry: *"the same commit thou to faithful men"*
  (2 Timothy 2:2). Paul's life is still preaching through every Timothy since.
- **Elijah and Elisha** — Elijah called Elisha from the plow (1 Kings 19:19), poured years
  into him, and left him a double portion of his spirit (2 Kings 2:9). The mentor's
  ministry did not end at the chariot of fire; it *doubled.*
- **Jesus and the Twelve** — the Lord could have dazzled the world alone. Instead He
  invested in a handful of ordinary men and commanded them to "make disciples of all
  nations" (Matthew 28:19). We are the fruit of that multiplication today.

---

### Illustration
Hold a single apple in your hand and you can count the seeds.

But no one can count the apples in a single seed.

One seed buried in the ground becomes a tree; the tree bears a hundred apples; each apple
hides a dozen more seeds — and in twenty years, one apple has become an orchard.

A person who only eats the apple gets one piece of fruit.

A person who *plants the seed* gets a forest he will never finish counting.

**A modern picture:** A candle loses nothing by lighting another candle — yet a room once
dark can blaze with light from a single flame passed hand to hand. **Pour your life into
others, and your light keeps burning in rooms you will never enter.**

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Name one person you could pour into — and reach out to them this week. Don't wait to feel
  qualified; you only need to be one step ahead.
- Write down three things God has taught you that you want to make sure get passed on.
- Pray for a "Timothy" by name — ask God to show you who to invest your life in.

---

### Reflection Questions
- When I am gone, what will keep going because I poured it into a person?
- Am I trying to be irreplaceable, or am I making myself reproducible?

### Longevity Insight
> Researchers studying the world's longest-living communities keep finding the same thing:
> the elders are not set aside — they are *engaged*, teaching, mentoring, and pouring into
> the young. Generativity, the psychologists call it: investing in those who come after
> you. It adds years to the giver and life to the receiver. God designed it that way. The
> life that pours itself out into others is the life that refuses to die.
