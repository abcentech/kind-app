# EPISODE 23
## Satisfied with Long Life

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 5: Finishing (*Leaving a Legacy*)

### Key Scripture
> "With long life will I satisfy him, and shew him my salvation."
> — Psalm 91:16 (KJV)

---

### Hook
We have come to the end of the journey.

For an entire month we have walked through the secrets of a long, fruitful life —

through Foundation,

through Faith,

through Focus,

through Fitness,

and now to the Finish.

And here, at the last episode, God brings us back to where we began:

to one verse —

one promise —

that has been the heartbeat of this whole series.

*"With long life will I satisfy him, and shew him my salvation."*

This is not just how a life gets *longer.*

This is how a life ends **satisfied.**

---

### Big Idea
> The true secret of longevity is not merely to live long, but to reach the end satisfied —
> with no regret, seeing God's salvation, and hearing "Well done."

---

### Why This Matters
A person can live a hundred years and reach the end empty.

And a person can be deeply satisfied — full, complete, at peace — because they spent their
years on the only thing that lasts.

God's promise in Psalm 91:16 has two halves, and we must not separate them.

He does not merely promise *length* of days. He promises to *satisfy.* And He defines that
satisfaction by what comes next: *"and shew him my salvation."*

Long life without salvation is just a long road to an empty end. But a life given to God is
a life that arrives **full.**

---

### Teaching

#### 1. God's Promise Is Satisfaction, Not Just Length
Look carefully: God does not say, *"With long life will I satisfy his calendar."* He says,
*"With long life will I satisfy *him*"* — the person, the soul.

You can have many years and be unsatisfied. You can have wealth, success, and a long life,
and still echo Solomon's verdict: *"vanity of vanities; all is vanity"* (Ecclesiastes
1:2).

The years are the gift. *Satisfaction* is the promise. And satisfaction is found only in
the One who gave the years.

**Application:** Stop chasing a longer life as the goal. Chase the God who satisfies, and
let Him give the years as He sees fit.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 91:16; Psalm 63:5; Ecclesiastes 1:2

#### 2. The End Goal Is Seeing His Salvation
The verse does not end with "long life." It ends with *"and shew him my salvation."*

This is the destination of the whole series. Every secret — feeding the spirit, renewing
the mind, stewarding the body, finishing the race — points to *this*: that you would see,
know, and rest in the salvation of God in Jesus Christ.

Old Simeon, holding the infant Jesus, said it perfectly: *"Lord, now lettest thou thy
servant depart in peace... for mine eyes have seen thy salvation"* (Luke 2:29–30). He could
die satisfied — not because he had lived long, but because he had seen the Savior.

**Application:** Make sure of your salvation in Christ above everything else. No length of
days is worth anything if you do not arrive having seen His salvation.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Luke 2:25–32; Psalm 91:16; John 3:16

#### 3. Looking Back Without Regret
The most satisfied lives are not the ones with no sorrow. They are the ones with no regret —
lives poured out for what truly mattered.

Jesus, on the eve of the cross, could pray: *"I have glorified thee on the earth: I have
finished the work which thou gavest me to do"* (John 17:4). No unfinished assignment. No
wasted years. No regret.

David, at the end, "served his own generation by the will of God" and then "fell on sleep"
(Acts 13:36). He looked back on a life spent on God's purpose.

A satisfied old age is built by a thousand right choices made while there was still time.

**Application:** Live today the way you will wish you had lived when you look back. Spend
your years on what you will not regret.

**Supporting Scriptures:** John 17:4; Acts 13:36; 2 Corinthians 5:9–10

#### 4. Hearing "Well Done, Good and Faithful Servant"
There is one verdict every other verdict bows to. Not the eulogy. Not the obituary. Not the
opinions of people who watched your life.

It is the voice of the Master saying: *"Well done, thou good and faithful servant... enter
thou into the joy of thy lord"* (Matthew 25:21).

Notice what He commends: not *successful* — *faithful.* Not *famous* — *good and faithful.*
The reward of a long, well-spent life is not applause; it is *His* approval, and entrance
into *His* joy.

That is the satisfaction Psalm 91:16 promises — to hear those words at the end, and to step
into the joy of the Lord forever.

**Application:** Live for one audience and one verdict. Order this day so that, on the last
day, you would hear "Well done."

**Supporting Scriptures:** Matthew 25:21; 1 Corinthians 4:2; Revelation 2:10

---

### Biblical Example
- **Abraham** "died in a good old age, an old man, and full of years" (Genesis 25:8) — the
  Hebrew literally says *satisfied with years.* He saw the promise begin and died at peace.
- **Job**, after all his suffering, "died, being old and full of days" (Job 42:17) —
  restored, satisfied, having seen God with his own eyes (Job 42:5).
- **Simeon** held the baby Jesus and was ready to depart in peace, *"for mine eyes have seen
  thy salvation"* (Luke 2:29–30). His satisfaction was the Savior Himself.
- **Jesus**, the perfect finisher, declared, *"It is finished"* (John 19:30), having
  glorified the Father and finished the work — the foundation of every satisfied life since.

Each one reached the end not merely old, but *full* — satisfied, and having seen God's
salvation.

---

### Illustration
Picture a farmer at the end of harvest.

For months he rose before dawn. He broke the hard ground, planted in hope, watered in
drought, watched the sky, pulled the weeds, and trusted God for the increase.

Now he stands at the edge of the field in the golden evening light.

The barns are full. The work is done. His hands are calloused and his back is bent —

but his face is at peace.

He does not say, *"I wish I had rested more."* He says, *"It was worth every season."*

**A modern picture:** Think of the difference between a tourist and a pilgrim at the end of
a long road. The tourist asks, *"Was it comfortable?"* The pilgrim asks, *"Did I arrive?"*
A life lived for Christ arrives. **At the end of the road stands not an empty horizon, but a
Father, and a "Well done," and joy that never ends.**

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Make sure of your salvation in Jesus Christ — the one thing the whole series has been
  pointing you to. If you have never trusted Him, do it today.
- Live this day so that, looking back from the end, you would have no regret about it.
- Order one decision today by the only verdict that lasts: *Would this earn a "Well done"?*

### Reflection Questions
- If my life ended today, would I be satisfied — having seen God's salvation?
- What am I spending my years on that I might one day regret? What would I never regret?
- Am I living for the applause of people, or for the "Well done" of God?

> heart of it: He does not merely offer length of days; He offers *satisfaction* — a life
> that arrives full and sees His salvation. The longest life is not the one with the most
> years. It is the one that ends able to say, *"It was worth every season — and I have seen
> the Lord."*

### Final Charge
We began with a single promise — *"With long life will I satisfy him, and shew him my
salvation"* (Psalm 91:16) — and we have traveled the Five F's to reach it.

**Foundation. Faith. Focus. Fitness. Finishing.**

Life begins with God. A strong spirit sustains it. A renewed mind preserves it. A
well-stewarded body carries it. And a faithful finish crowns it.

But hear this on the last day of the journey: the goal was never simply to add more years
to your life. Many people live long and arrive empty. The goal — the true **Secret of
Longevity** — is to fill every year you are given with the One who gives them.

So whatever decade you are in — your first or your eighth — do not merely *spend* it. *Pour*
it out. Build on the foundation. Strengthen your spirit. Renew your mind. Honor the temple.
Finish your race. Multiply your life into others. And when your last day comes, may you lay
down the baton having heard the only words that matter:

> *"Well done, thou good and faithful servant... enter thou into the joy of thy lord."*
> — Matthew 25:21

**The true secret of longevity is this: to live every decade — every season, every single
day — for the glory of Jesus Christ.**

Run well. Finish strong. See you at the line.
