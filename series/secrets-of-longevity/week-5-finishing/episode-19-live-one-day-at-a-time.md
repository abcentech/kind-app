# EPISODE 19
## Live One Day at a Time

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 5: Finishing (*Leaving a Legacy*)

### Key Scripture
> "Take therefore no thought for the morrow: for the morrow shall take thought for the
> things of itself. Sufficient unto the day is the evil thereof."
> — Matthew 6:34 (KJV)

---

### Hook
No one has ever lived a single year all at once.

No one has ever lived a whole decade in a day.

A long life is not given to you in bulk.

It is handed to you the same way bread was given in the wilderness —

one morning at a time.

You don't get tomorrow's strength today.

You get *today's* strength, today.

---

### Big Idea
> A faithful life is not built in great leaps; it is built one obedient day at a time.

---

### Why This Matters
We worry about a future we cannot live in yet.

We carry tomorrow's burden on today's shoulders — and break under weight God never asked
us to lift.

God does not give grace for an imagined tomorrow.

He gives grace for the day you are actually in.

If you want to finish a long race, stop trying to run all the miles at once. **Run the
mile you are on.**

---

### Teaching

#### 1. God Gives Strength for the Day, Not the Decade
When Moses blessed the tribe of Asher, he gave a promise that covers every season of a
long life: *"as thy days, so shall thy strength be."*

Notice — not *"as thy years."* As thy *days*.

God matches His strength to the day in front of you.

He has never promised tomorrow's grace today, because you do not need it today. When
tomorrow comes, the grace will be there — fresh, exactly sufficient, like manna on the
ground.

This is why anxious people grow tired so quickly: they are spending today's strength
trying to carry next month's load.

**Application:** Stop asking God for strength for a future you cannot see. Ask Him for
strength for the day you are standing in — and trust Him to meet you again tomorrow.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Deuteronomy 33:25; Lamentations 3:22–23; Exodus 16:4

#### 2. Faithfulness Is a Daily Word, Not a Lifetime Word
We admire people who were "faithful to the end," but no one is faithful to the end who was
not first faithful *today*.

A finished course is just a long string of single days, each one chosen well.

Jesus taught us to pray for *daily* bread — not a month's supply, not a guarantee against
ever being hungry again. Daily dependence keeps us close to God.

You cannot obey God for next year. You can only obey Him today. So the whole question of a
faithful life narrows down to one small, repeatable thing: *Will I walk with God today?*

**Application:** Make one specific act of obedience today — not a grand resolution for the
rest of your life. Today's obedience is the only obedience you can actually give.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Matthew 6:11; Luke 9:23; Psalm 118:24

#### 3. Worry Steals from the Life God Gave You
Jesus' command in Matthew 6:34 is not a suggestion to relax. It is a rescue from a thief.

Worry does not empty tomorrow of its trouble; it empties *today* of its strength.

It borrows pain that may never arrive and pays interest with peace you can never get back.

The anxious heart wears out the body. The trusting heart is renewed. This is not only
spiritual wisdom — it is one of the quiet secrets of a long, unhurried life.

**Application:** When a tomorrow-worry shows up today, hand it back to God in prayer the
moment you notice it. Refuse to live a day you have not yet been given.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Matthew 6:25–34; Philippians 4:6–7; 1 Peter 5:7

#### 4. Today Is the Only Day You Can Honor God In
Yesterday is sealed. Tomorrow is unborn.

The only place you can actually love God, serve people, and do right is **here, now,
today.**

A legacy is not built on the day you die. It is built on ten thousand ordinary days when
no one was watching and you chose to be faithful anyway.

The Scripture warns us not even to boast of tomorrow, "for ye know not what shall be on
the morrow" (James 4:14). Tomorrow is God's. Today is your assignment.

**Application:** Treat this single day as a complete unit of obedience. Ask: *If this were
the only day I had to honor God, would today count?*

**Supporting Scriptures:** James 4:13–15; Psalm 90:12; Hebrews 3:13

---

### Biblical Example
- **Israel in the wilderness** gathered manna *every morning*. Those who tried to hoard it
  for tomorrow found it bred worms and stank (Exodus 16:4, 20). God was teaching a nation
  to live one day at a time.
- **Daniel** prayed *three times a day* (Daniel 6:10) — not in one great heroic stretch,
  but in a daily rhythm that carried him through decades, even into a den of lions.
- **Jesus** rose "a great while before day" to pray (Mark 1:35), facing each day's
  assignment with fresh strength drawn that morning from the Father.

The longest-finishing lives in Scripture were built the same way yours must be: daily.

---

### Illustration
Watch how a great cathedral is built.

No one lays a thousand stones in an afternoon.

A mason lays one stone, sets it true, checks the line — and lays the next.

For years. For a lifetime.

He may not live to see the spire. But every stone he set was straight, and the building
rose because of faithful, ordinary days.

**A modern picture:** Compound interest. A small deposit made *daily* outgrows a fortune
gambled all at once. The bank of heaven works the same way — small, daily acts of
obedience compounding into a finished life. **You are not behind. You just have today's
stone to lay.**

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Name one thing God is asking of you *today* — and do that one thing.
- The next time you feel anxious, locate the worry: is it a *today* problem or a
  *tomorrow* problem? Hand every tomorrow back to God.
- End the day with a single sentence of thanks: *"Lord, You gave me strength for today."*

---

### Reflection Questions
- Am I carrying tomorrow's weight on today's strength?
- If I am only ever asked to be faithful *today*, what does faithfulness look like in the
  next twelve hours?

---

### Prayer
Father,

Thank You for the gift of this single day.

I will not borrow tomorrow's troubles, and I will not waste today's strength on them.

Give me grace for today. Give me bread for today. Give me obedience for today.

As my days are, so let my strength be — fresh from Your hand each morning.

Teach me to build a faithful life one ordinary day at a time, until the last day is done.

In Jesus' Name,

Amen.

### Longevity Insight
> Doctors who study stress have a name for it: *anticipatory anxiety* — the body pouring
> out stress hormones over events that have not happened and often never will. It wears
> the heart, the gut, and the mind. Jesus prescribed the cure two thousand years ago:
> *take no thought for the morrow.* People who learn to live in the present day sleep
> better, age slower, and finish stronger. God gives strength by the day — and the day is
> always enough.
