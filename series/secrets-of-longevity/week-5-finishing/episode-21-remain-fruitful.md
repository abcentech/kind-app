# EPISODE 21
## Remain Fruitful

> **Series:** Secrets of Longevity — Week 5: Finishing (*Leaving a Legacy*)

### Key Scripture
> "The righteous shall flourish like the palm tree: he shall grow like a cedar in Lebanon.
> ...They shall still bring forth fruit in old age; they shall be fat and flourishing."
> — Psalm 92:12, 14 (KJV)

---

### Hook
The world tells you that fruitfulness has a deadline.

Be useful while you're young.

Produce while you're strong.

Then step aside, sit down, and fade.

But God never said that.

God planted a different picture in His Word —

a tree that does not wither with age,

a tree that bears its *best* fruit in its *oldest* season.

In the kingdom of God, your last decades are not your least.

---

### Big Idea
> In God's economy, growing older is meant to increase your fruit, not end it.

---

### Why This Matters
Many believers quietly retire from usefulness long before they retire from breathing.

They assume their fruitful years are behind them.

But Psalm 92 dismantles that assumption completely: the righteous *"shall still bring forth
fruit in old age."*

Not *some* fruit. Not *leftover* fruit. Fruit so abundant the psalmist calls them "fat and
flourishing."

If you belong to God, your most fruitful season may not be behind you. It may be ahead.

---

### Teaching

#### 1. Righteousness, Not Age, Determines Fruitfulness
Notice *who* keeps bearing fruit: "*the righteous* shall flourish."

The secret of lifelong fruitfulness is not a strong body or a young face. It is a right
relationship with God, maintained over decades.

The palm tree and the cedar are both evergreen — they do not shed their strength with the
seasons. Why? Because, as the next verse says, they are *"planted in the house of the
LORD"* (Psalm 92:13). Their roots are in the right soil.

A tree planted in God does not run out of seasons.

**Application:** Don't measure your future usefulness by your age or your energy. Tend your
roots. Stay planted in the house of the Lord, and the fruit will keep coming.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 92:12–14; Psalm 1:3; Jeremiah 17:7–8

#### 2. Abiding Is the Source of Fruit
Jesus said it plainly: *"He that abideth in me, and I in him, the same bringeth forth much
fruit: for without me ye can do nothing"* (John 15:5).

Fruit is not produced by striving. It is produced by *abiding* — staying connected to the
Vine.

This is wonderful news for the aging believer. Your arms may grow weaker, but your
*abiding* can grow deeper. And the deeper the abiding, the greater the fruit.

A branch does not strain to produce grapes. It simply stays attached, and the life of the
vine does the rest.

**Application:** Make abiding — daily, unhurried time in God's presence — the priority of
your later years. The fruit follows the abiding, not the effort.

**Supporting Scriptures:** John 15:4–8; Psalm 1:2–3; Colossians 1:10

#### 3. The Fruit of Age Is Often the Sweetest
Some fruit only ripens slowly. Wisdom. Patience. Perseverance. A tested faith. A long
record of God's faithfulness that younger believers have not yet lived to see.

These are the fruits that age *alone* can produce. The young cannot offer them, because
they have not yet walked the years.

The grey head is "a crown of glory, if it be found in the way of righteousness"
(Proverbs 16:31). Your years are not a liability to apologize for; they are a harvest to
give away.

**Application:** Identify the fruit only your years could have grown — your hard-won wisdom,
your testimony of God's faithfulness — and deliberately give it to someone younger.

**Supporting Scriptures:** Proverbs 16:31; Job 12:12; Titus 2:3–5

#### 4. Increasing Influence, Not Ending Influence
The cedar of Lebanon does not shrink as it ages — it grows *taller*, its roots reach
*deeper*, and its shade stretches *wider*. The older the tree, the greater its reach.

So it should be with the believer. The years should widen your influence, not retire it.
You have more story, more wisdom, more spiritual authority, more people who trust you than
you did at twenty-five.

The enemy wants you to believe your influence is shrinking. God says it should be growing.

**Application:** Refuse to step back from influence God has given you to grow. Ask, *"How
can I use my voice, my years, and my testimony for greater impact, not less?"*

**Supporting Scriptures:** Psalm 92:12; Proverbs 4:18; Isaiah 46:4

---

### Biblical Example
- **Moses** was eighty when God called him to lead a nation — and at one hundred and
  twenty "his eye was not dim, nor his natural force abated" (Deuteronomy 34:7). His most
  fruitful decades were his last forty years.
- **Anna** the prophetess was about eighty-four, yet she *"departed not from the temple,
  but served God with fastings and prayers night and day"* (Luke 2:36–38) — and she was
  among the first to recognize and proclaim the infant Christ. Her oldest season was her
  most fruitful.
- **Caleb** at eighty-five declared, *"as yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day
  that Moses sent me"* — and asked for a mountain to conquer (Joshua 14:10–11).

These were not surviving the end of life. They were *flourishing* in it.

---

### Illustration
Most fruit trees slow down as they age.

But the olive tree does the opposite.

Some olive trees in the Holy Land are over a thousand years old — gnarled, ancient,
hollow-trunked — and they are *still bearing olives.* In fact, the oldest trees often yield
the richest oil.

The years did not exhaust them. The years *deepened* them. Their roots went so far down
that no drought could finish them.

**A modern picture:** A fine wine, a seasoned violin, a cast-iron pan — some things only
get *better* with age. God designed a category of fruit that only the long years can
ripen. **Don't despise your seasons. You may be entering your richest harvest.**

---

### Application
**What should I do today?**

- Reject the lie that your fruitful years are over — say out loud, *"I shall still bring
  forth fruit."*
- Tend your roots today: spend unhurried time abiding in Christ, the source of all fruit.
- Identify one piece of fruit only your years have grown — and give it away to someone this
  week.

---

### Reflection Questions
- Have I quietly retired from usefulness while I still have breath?
- What fruit is God still wanting to grow through me in this season of my life?

### Longevity Insight
> Foresters know a strange truth: many trees keep growing their entire lives. A tree never
> reaches an age where it decides to stop. As long as the roots draw water, the tree adds
> rings. Scientists who study human aging see something similar — people who stay engaged,
> learning, and contributing into their later years stay sharper and live longer. God said
> it first in Psalm 92: the righteous *"shall still bring forth fruit in old age."* Stay
> rooted, keep abiding, and keep bearing.
